On January 1, 1914, British forces captured Le Sars and Ovillers in France, while French forces captured Mora in Kamerun. The same day, Germany was blockaded in the Atlantic Ocean and Mediterranean Sea. As the war progressed, British forces clashed with German troops in Africa, Togoland, and Kamerun. In August, the Battle of Agbeluvhoe took place in Togoland, and the Siege of Mora began in Kamerun. The British launched a surprise attack on Penang in October 1914. The following year, the Second Battle of Garua occurred in Kamerun, and the Baralong incidents involved British and German U-boats in the Atlantic Ocean. In 1916, the Battle of Jutland took place in the North Sea, and the Lochnagar mine was planted in France. The Capture of Martinpuich and Eaucourt l'Abbaye occurred in France, while the British launched Operation Alberich in France. In 1917, the Capture of Wurst Farm took place in Belgium, and the Second Battle of Heligoland Bight occurred in the North Sea. The following year, the Imperial German Navy launched a submarine attack on the Portuguese steamer São Miguel and the naval trawler NRP Augusto de Castilho in the Atlantic Ocean.
